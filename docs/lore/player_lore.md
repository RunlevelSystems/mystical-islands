# Player Lore
## The Shattered Crown Isles

Sailors still call the archipelago the **Shattered Crown Isles**, though no two charts agree on where the old crown truly fell. Some say the islands were once the peaks of a single empire. Others whisper that the sea swallowed a kingdom in one terrible night and left only broken stones, haunted lights, and half-remembered names behind.

Most folk know only this: the world was not always so divided.

Now the seas between the islands are cruel and hungry. Storm walls form without warning, strange lights drift beneath the waves, and ships vanish along routes that were safe the season before. Even so, merchants, pilgrims, scavengers, soldiers, and treasure hunters continue to sail. Every island promises something the others cannot offer—fortune, refuge, forgotten power, or answers no scholar has yet dared to speak aloud.

## What the People Believe

Across taverns, shrines, and dockside markets, a few stories are repeated more than most:

- the world was broken by divine wrath or royal betrayal
- the ruins were built by giants, sorcerer-kings, or a vanished elder race
- witches learned their craft by bargaining with the forests themselves
- portals are cursed thresholds that open only when the dead are restless
- sea monsters guard drowned cities below the waves
- the deepest storms hide islands that appear only for the chosen or the doomed
- relics taken from old ruins bring either great fortune or long, patient ruin

No one agrees on which version is true, and perhaps that uncertainty is what keeps the old stories alive.

## Life After the Breaking

For common people, history matters less than survival.

- fisher fleets watch the tides for unnatural changes
- caravans barter salvage, timber, ore, herbs, and preserved food between harbors
- captains hire mercenaries before venturing into contested waters
- shrinekeepers bless anchors, maps, and lanterns before dangerous voyages
- frontier settlers build around old stones, hoping whatever protected them once still lingers

Civilization survives by clinging to the few routes that remain dependable.

## Rumors of the Isles

### The Mainland Kingdom

The mainland is said to be the last great bastion of order: crowded docks, noble houses, busy markets, and enough soldiers to keep worse things at bay. Yet even there, rumors spread faster than law. Courtiers speak of missing archives, sealed vaults beneath the capital, and advisors who fund expeditions for reasons they never share.

### Witchwood Isle

Witchwood is feared as much as it is desired. Hunters claim the forest rearranges itself by moonlight. Elven enclaves vanish behind living boughs, and the witches of the deep groves are said to know the price of every secret before it is asked.

### Frostpeak Isle

Frostpeak rises above the sea like a broken blade of ice and stone. Dwarven halls endure beneath the mountain, but even seasoned miners speak softly of sealed caverns, sleeping things under the rock, and fire seen behind frozen walls.

### The Ashen Deadlands

The Deadlands are cursed in every tongue. Ash falls like snow. Broken fortresses smolder without flame. Raiders, necromancers, and grave-marked warbands battle over ruins that few can hold for long.

### The Shattered Reefs

The Reefs are lawless, profitable, and full of places too narrow for navy ships to follow. Smugglers disappear into canyon coves, pirates trade stolen charts in lantern-lit ports, and treasure hunters chase wreck rumors across red stone and hidden oases.

### Stormreach Isles

Beyond the safer routes lie the Stormreach Isles, where the sky never truly clears. Many believe the storms there are alive. Few return from those waters unchanged, and the ones who do bring stories of broken towers, impossible lights, and creatures that should not exist.

## Adventurer Outlook

To an adventurer, the archipelago offers more than danger.

- lost shrines and sealed chambers promise relics and rare materials
- hidden coves and wrecks reward skilled navigation
- factions pay well for knowledge, recovered artifacts, and monster trophies
- expedition crews can earn fame by opening routes others fear to chart
- the farther one sails from safety, the more the world seems willing to reveal

Every voyage begins with a map, a crew, and a rumor. The brave call that opportunity. The cautious call it the first line of an epitaph.
