# Artifacts & Objects
## Purpose

Discoverable objects should connect exploration, progression, faction rivalry, and worldbuilding. Items found in Mystical Isles are not just loot drops; they are evidence of lost systems, vanished expeditions, and ongoing competition over the past.

## World Integration

### Ancient Relics
Ancient relics should feel culturally contested. One faction may want them studied, another displayed as proof of legitimacy, and another hidden before they destabilize a region.

### Magical Artifacts
These items are how players experience mystery on the surface layer of the setting. Internally, many are dimensional tools or charged remnants, but their presentation can remain wondrous and dangerous.

### Lost Technology and Ancient Machines
These objects connect directly to internal lore. They can restore wards, reactivate transit nodes, unlock sealed chambers, or shift a dungeon from dormant to active.

### Cursed and Legendary Items
High-risk objects should create meaningful trade-offs: stronger short-term power, greater faction suspicion, increased corruption exposure, or complicated quest consequences.

## Discovery Mechanics

- environmental puzzles and landmark clues
- treasure maps with partial coordinates
- faction-sponsored expeditions
- rotating anomaly events and storm windows
- boss trophies that combine into unlock items
- ruin restoration chains that reveal deeper layers over time

## Rarity Use

- **Common-Uncommon:** support recurring exploration loops and crafting systems
- **Rare-Epic:** drive named quest chains, ship progression, and faction negotiation
- **Legendary:** reshape regional access, boss encounters, or late-game world-state objectives
