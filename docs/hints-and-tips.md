# Hints and Tips

## Loading Screen & Gameplay Tips

1. Watch shoreline caves at low tide; hidden entrances often open briefly.
2. Harbor masters usually hint at the safest route for your current level.
3. If a monster howls, nearby packs may join the fight.
4. Carry antidotes before entering swamp or jungle zones.
5. Treasure chests in cursed ruins may be mimics.
6. Repair gear before long sea routes to avoid mid-expedition failures.
7. Faction reputation can unlock cheaper travel and unique merchants.
8. Night travel increases ambush risk on pirate-controlled waters.
9. Gathering nodes respawn fastest in regions with active trade caravans.
10. Some docks only appear after specific world-state events.
11. Undead resist fear but are vulnerable to holy effects.
12. Dragons punish clustered groups; spread out during breath attacks.
13. Use terrain choke points to control goblin swarms.
14. Weather effects can reveal otherwise hidden runic markers.
15. Always stock food and bandages before expedition chains.
16. Black market prices improve after repeated successful contraband runs.
17. If a harbor is blockaded, ask smugglers for alternate routes.
18. Ancient ruins often hide vertical paths above the main chamber.
19. Sea monsters target damaged ships first.
20. Bring mining tools to Frostfang; rare ore veins appear in side caverns.
21. Listen for whisper audio cues in Veil zones; they warn of anomalies.
22. Potion cooldown management is as important as raw damage.
23. Use local chat in crowded ports to recruit quickly for voyages.
24. Some elite monsters change abilities at low health.
25. Keep one utility weapon for enemies resistant to your main damage type.
26. Guild rank often controls access to high-value quartermasters.
27. In desert islands, movement skills can save you from hazard zones.
28. Corrupted creatures may leave lingering ground effects after death.
29. Check tavern noticeboards for rotating regional contracts.
30. Trade surplus crafting materials before crossing into high-tax ports.
31. If your inventory is full, rare drops can be lost during long grinds.
32. Escort events are safer with at least one control-oriented class.
33. Treant enemies can root entire groups; bring cleanse abilities.
34. Dungeon side rooms frequently hold keys for shortcut gates.
35. Use /loc before reporting bugs so GMs can reproduce the issue.
36. Ship supplies are cheaper at production islands than capital ports.
37. Frost-based enemies often apply movement slows; pack cleansing tools.
38. Siren encounters are easier when players spread and interrupt quickly.
39. Cave systems on Stoneclaw frequently connect to hidden smuggler exits.
40. Quest chains involving relics often branch by faction alignment.
41. Save high-tier reagents for crafted upgrades, not early prototypes.
42. If mobs leash unexpectedly, check pathing around elevation changes.
43. Witch zones often reward resistance-focused gear over pure damage.
44. Port rumors can foreshadow upcoming world events.
45. Blackreef contracts pay more at night but attract stronger enemies.
46. Use map markers for multi-island crafting routes.
47. Late-game Veil areas demand balanced resistances, not single-stat stacking.
48. Boss telegraphs are easier to read with camera zoom pulled back.
49. Harbor route unlocks are a major progression milestone.
50. Keep a reserve of travel currency for emergency returns.
51. Corrupted dragons can mutate patterns between encounters.
52. Guild storage helps stabilize crafting economies during progression spikes.
53. Relic buyers pay best for unidentified artifacts in pristine condition.
54. Player-built settlements draw regional attacks if defenses are neglected.
55. Always scout pirate coves before committing large cargo runs.
56. Some world bosses spawn only after sequential regional events complete.
57. Use gathering downtime to train secondary skill lines.
58. Carry light sources in crypt networks with visibility penalties.
59. Faction uniforms can influence NPC dialogue and access checks.
60. When in doubt, regroup at a harbor hub and re-plan your route.
